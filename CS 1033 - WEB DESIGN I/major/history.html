History of Hawaii

The Hawaiin islands were first discovered by the Polynesians in the year 400 CE, when they courageously traveled in canoes. It was only until 1778 when Europeans discovered the islands in a mission led by Captain James Cook. Between 1791 and 1810, King Kamehameha defeated all other rulers and united all the islands. It was shortly after that when Christian missionaries, traders, and whalers arrived, wiping out more than a quarter of the local population due to the many diseases the Europeans brought with them. The United States of America annexed Hawaii as a territory in 1898 as they already controlled their sugar-based economy. 

 

 

The Hawaiin Islands were formed 70 million years ago by a large chain of around 80 submarine volcanoes. So we we stand on top of extinct volcanoes! Some of these volcanos are active (6 total active), including the youngest, Kilauea, which erupts almost continuously. Mauna Lau (located on the Hawai'i island) is the largest volcano in the world, which has erupted 33 times since 1843. While Mauna Kea is not so active, it is actually considered to be the tallest mountain in the world if measuring from the base which is underwater!

 

 

 

Islands 

There are 8 main islands of Hawaii. These are:

  • Hawai'i
  • Maui
  • Koho'olawe
  • Moloka'i
  • Lana'i
  • O'ahu
  • Kaua'i
  • Ni'ihau

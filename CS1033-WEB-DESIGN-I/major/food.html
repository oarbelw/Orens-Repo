Hawaiin Cuisine

Hawaii's rich culture, diversity and history is reflected in the unique local food. Your tastebuds will thank you for making the call to travel to one of the islands! Here are some of the favourite dishes to the locals. 

 

Poke

This delicious seafood meal is a twist on Japan's sashimi (raw fish). The most common fish used, Ahi Tuna, is served in small cubes among many other ingredients in a bowl. Usually, other ingredients include many vegetables, rice, and anything else you want!

Poi

Poi is a traditional starch dish that is a Hawaiin staple. It is a thick paste made from taro root that is steamed or baked and then pounded. So fun!

Laulau

Laulau again uses the Taro, but this time, its leaves. This dish wraps (usually) pork in layers of its leaves and then cooks everything in an underground hot rock oven for hours until ready.

Kalua Pig

This Hawaiin spin to preparing pork consists of slow roasting pork for hours in an underground hot rock oven, giving it a rich smoky flavour. Best served with rice!
